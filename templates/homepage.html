Crop-Yield-Prediction

This project is established towards the fulfilment of my masters degree at the university of Sunderland on a course titled data science product development(CETM46). It is an agricultural data science products that entails the design, development, and management process used in “crop yield prediction”. Crop yield prediction is a difficult task in the field of agriculture and is crucial for decision-making at the local, regional, and global scales. These predictions rely on various factors such as climate, soil, environmental, and crop parameters. Decision support models are widely used in extracting important crop features for prediction. These extractions require the use of sensing technologies, variable rate technologies, management information systems, and responses to variations within and between cropping systems. Machine learning (ML) models were also used after processing agricultural data and Regression models were deployed to predict the yield.

Dataset

Fertilizer usage and export quantity data which is in correspondence with yield data was taken from kaggle database. It utilizes weather conditions and factors such as Temperature(avg), precitipation, Export Quantity, and Fertilizer Used were used to train the ML models.

ML Model

In order to predict the crop yield for each crop, regression models were deployed.

Explanation of the Parameters

Temperature (Avg): High temperature considerably influences the crops by affecting several physiological injuries like leaf abscission, leaf scorching, senescence, and root and shoot growth limitation that subsequently leads to a reduction in yield.

Precipitation: It can be defined as earthward falling of water drops or ice particles that have formed by rapid condensation in the atmosphere.

Export Quantity: Export crops means crops whose majority of production shall be for export outside the State or whose majority of the value-added products shall be exported.

Fertilizer Usage: Most fertilizers that are commonly used in agriculture contain the three basic plant nutrients: nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Some fertilizers also contain certain "micronutrients," such as zinc and other metals, that are necessary for plant growth.
